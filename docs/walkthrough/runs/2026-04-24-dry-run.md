## Harness validation summary

End-to-end on Provost in ~15 minutes of driving across two CDP-attached Chromes. The agent-browser primitives that worked reliably for this audit:

- `agent-browser connect <port>` + `agent-browser close` to swap sessions (fast, ~1s).
- `agent-browser open <url>` for direct route navigation (no `wait --load networkidle`).
- `agent-browser eval "(()=>{...})()"` to extract structured assertions (counts, regex matches, headings, body text). This is the workhorse.
- `agent-browser snapshot -i -c` for first-time exploration of a page's interactive structure.
- `agent-browser errors` to confirm no JS errors (almost always empty here, which is the pass state).

Don'ts confirmed by trial-and-error:
- ❌ Never `wait --load networkidle` on Provost (Convex websocket → never idle).
- ❌ Don't combine `--session <name>` with `--cdp <port>` — wedges the daemon.
- ❌ After `pkill agent-browser-darwin-arm64`, always `rm -f ~/.agent-browser/*.{pid,sock,stream,version,engine}` before reconnecting; stale sockets cause silent hangs.
